\begin{Claim}
For all $n \in$\mathbb{N}, $n^2+6n+7 < 20n^2$ \\
\end{Claim}
\begin{PBI}
Base Case, (n=1) $1^2+(6)1+7=14<20=(10)1=(20)1^2$ \\
Inductive Step:
Let n be a natural number. Assume $n^2+6n+7<20n^2$\\
$(n+1)^2+6(n+1)+7=n^2+2n+1+6n+6+7=n^2+6n+7+2n+7<10n^2+2n+7$ \\
\begin{align*}
&20n^2+2n+7 \\
&<20n^2+40n+7 \\
&<20n^2+40n+20 \\
&=20(n^2+2n+1) \\
&=20(n+1)^2 \\
\end{align*}
So $(n+1)^2+6(n+1)+7<20(n+1)^2$ By proof of mathematical inductionm this concludes the proof. \\
\end{PBI}
